Did You Know?

Who founded and named Abbottabad?

Abbottabad is a city in the Hazara region of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, in north-eastern Pakistan. It is 120 kilometres (74 mi) north of the capital Islamabad. It was founded by Major James Abbott of the British Indian Army in 1853, who named it after himself.

Read More →
Did You Know?

Pakistan has the world's second-largest salt mine

The Khewra Salt Mine in Punjab, Pakistan, is the world's second-largest salt mine and one of the oldest. It has been mined since the 1200s and produces pink Himalayan salt exported globally.

Read More →
Did You Know?

Pakistan is home to K2, the world's most dangerous peak

K2 stands at 8,611 metres in the Karakoram range. It is considered harder and more deadly to climb than Everest, earning the nickname "The Savage Mountain."

Read More →
Did You Know?

The first Pakistani Nobel laureate was a physicist

Abdus Salam won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1979 for his contributions to the theory of the unified weak and electromagnetic interaction — the first Pakistani to receive a Nobel.
